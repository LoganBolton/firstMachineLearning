a young girl becomes a wartime marines penpal and when he visits at wars end expecting someone a bit more available comic complications ensue all ultimately works out well naturally but not before everyone involved has thoroughly chewed the scenery errol flynns deadon impression of humphrey bogart from casablanca is a highlight as are various sendups of his own swashbuckling image the jumping scene in the kitchen with forrest tucker is a riot it is tucker though who tucks the movie under his arm lowers his head and barrels over the goal line he demonstrates the comic flair more fully developed twenty years later in ftroop and imparts a liveliness and energy that flynn repeatedly plays off to raise his own performance eleanor parker does a fine job as the woman being pursued and little patti brady charms as tuckers actual penpal friend a fine lightweight coming home comedy in a genteel setting that children and romantics of all ages should find entertaining
